Privacy Policy — PvPeek

Last updated: 15 June 2026

In short: what's on your screen never leaves your phone. PvPeek reads a single screen frame only when you tap, analyses it entirely on your device, and never stores or sends any image or its text. It sends only limited diagnostics — crash reports and anonymous usage events — to help us fix bugs and improve accuracy. This notice is written to comply with major privacy laws, including the EU and UK GDPR, the Swiss FADP, and California's CCPA/CPRA.

Who's responsible

Diego Ballesteros, developer of PvPeek, is responsible for the data described here. For any privacy question or request, contact CONTACT_EMAIL_PLACEHOLDER.

What stays on your phone

To analyse a Pokémon, PvPeek captures one frame of your screen (only when you tap) and reads it on your device. The image and any text read from it are processed in memory and immediately discarded — never stored, uploaded, or shared. We don't collect screenshots, screen contents, your location, contacts, photos, or any account.

This data is processed on our behalf by Google (Firebase) and may be stored on its servers, including in the United States, under standard data-transfer safeguards. We do not sell or share your data or use it for advertising. We keep crash data for up to 90 days and usage data per Firebase's default retention (about 2 months for detailed events), and no longer than needed.

Your choices and rights

Wherever you live, we honour these rights for everyone: to access a copy of your data, to correct or delete it, to receive it in a portable form, and to object to or opt out of the diagnostics above. Sharing diagnostics is entirely optional — the app's analysis works without it. To make a request, just email us at the address above; we'll respond within the time your local law requires. If you're not happy with our response, you can complain to your local data-protection authority (for example, the ICO in the UK, the FDPIC in Switzerland, or your national authority in the EU). Because these diagnostics use device-level identifiers rather than your name, we may need a little information to find your data.

A few more things

We don't make automated decisions about you — the Pokémon analysis is just a calculation on your device. PvPeek isn't directed to children under 13, and we don't knowingly collect their data. We may update this policy; the date above will change and material updates will show in the app.
