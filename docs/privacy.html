Privacy Policy — PvPeek

Last updated: 15 June 2026

In short: what's on your screen never leaves your phone. PvPeek reads a single screen frame only when you tap, analyses it entirely on-device, and never stores or transmits any image or its text. It sends only limited diagnostics (crash reports and anonymous usage events) to help fix bugs and improve accuracy. This notice is written to meet the GDPR (EU), UK GDPR, Swiss FADP, CCPA/CPRA (California) and other US state laws, LGPD (Brazil), PIPEDA (Canada), and the Australian Privacy Principles.

Diego Ballesteros (developer of PvPeek)

We have not appointed a Data Protection Officer or representative, as we are not legally required to. For any privacy request, use the contact above.

2. On-device processing — not collected

To analyse a Pokémon, PvPeek captures one frame of your screen (via Android's screen-capture permission, only when you tap) and reads it on your device. The captured image and any text read from it are processed in memory and immediately discarded. They are never stored, uploaded, transmitted, or shared. We do not collect screenshots, screen recordings, the contents of your screen, your location, contacts, photos, or any account identifier.

3. Personal data we process, purposes, and legal basis

DataPurposeLegal basis (GDPR Art. 6)
Crash diagnostics — stack traces, device model, OS version, app version, and a Firebase installation identifier (Firebase Crashlytics) Detect and fix crashes; keep the app stable and secure Legitimate interests — Art. 6(1)(f)
Anonymous usage events — per analysis: outcome (success/failure), where the name was read from, number of types detected, whether CP was read, a device-resolution bucket, and the recognised Pokémon species name; plus a Firebase app-instance identifier and IP address (Google Analytics for Firebase) Understand which features and devices work, and improve reading accuracy Legitimate interests — Art. 6(1)(f)

Our legitimate interests are to diagnose crashes, secure the app, measure whether it reads correctly across devices, and improve it. You may object/opt out at any time (section 7).

4. Categories of personal information (CCPA/CPRA framing)

  • Identifiers — device/installation identifiers, app-instance identifier, IP address.
  • Internet/app activity — the anonymous in-app usage events listed above.
  • Geolocation — none (we do not collect location; IP is used only for routing/security by our processor).
  • Sensitive personal information — none collected.
  • Source — collected directly from your device via the app.

We do not sell your personal information and do not share it for cross-context behavioural advertising, as those terms are defined under the CCPA/CPRA. We have not sold or shared personal information in the preceding 12 months.

5. Recipients

Diagnostics are processed on our behalf by Google (Firebase Crashlytics and Google Analytics for Firebase), acting as our processor/service provider. We do not share your data with any other third party.

See Google's Privacy Policy: policies.google.com/privacy.

8. Retention

Crash diagnostics are retained by Firebase Crashlytics for up to 90 days. Analytics data is retained per Firebase's configured retention (by default up to 2 months for user/event-level data; aggregated reports may be kept longer). We keep personal data no longer than necessary for the purposes above.

9. Is providing this data required?

No. Providing diagnostics is not a statutory or contractual requirement and is not needed to use PvPeek's core features. The app's analysis works regardless; diagnostics only help us maintain and improve it, and you may object to or opt out of them.

10. Automated decision-making

We do not carry out automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you (GDPR Art. 22). The Pokémon analysis is a calculation performed on-device and is not used to make decisions about you.

11. Children

PvPeek is not directed to children under 13 (or the minimum age in your jurisdiction) and we do not knowingly collect their personal data (consistent with the US COPPA and equivalent laws). If you believe a child has provided data, contact us and we will delete it.
