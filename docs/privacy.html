Privacy Policy — PvPeek

Last updated: 15 June 2026

In short: what's on your screen never leaves your phone. PvPeek reads a single screen frame only when you tap, analyses it entirely on-device, and never stores or transmits any image or its text. It sends only limited diagnostics (crash reports and anonymous usage events) to help fix bugs and improve accuracy. This notice is provided under Article 13 of the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Who is responsible (data controller)

The controller responsible for processing your personal data is:

Diego Ballesteros (developer of PvPeek)
Contact: CONTACT_EMAIL_PLACEHOLDER

We have not appointed a Data Protection Officer, as we are not legally required to.

2. On-device processing — not collected

To analyse a Pokémon, PvPeek captures one frame of your screen (via Android's screen-capture permission, only when you tap) and reads it on your device. The captured image and any text read from it are processed in memory and immediately discarded. They are never stored, uploaded, transmitted, or shared. We do not collect screenshots, screen recordings, the contents of your screen, your location, contacts, photos, or any account identifier.

3. Personal data we process, purposes, and legal basis

DataPurposeLegal basis (GDPR Art. 6)
Crash diagnostics — stack traces, device model, OS version, app version, and a Firebase installation identifier (via Firebase Crashlytics) Detect and fix crashes; keep the app stable and secure Legitimate interests — Art. 6(1)(f)
Anonymous usage events — per analysis: outcome (success/failure), where the name was read from, number of types detected, whether CP was read, a device-resolution bucket, and the recognised Pokémon species name; plus a Firebase app-instance identifier and IP address (via Google Analytics for Firebase) Understand which features and devices work, and improve reading accuracy Legitimate interests — Art. 6(1)(f)

Our legitimate interests are to diagnose crashes, secure the app, measure whether it reads correctly across devices, and improve it. We have balanced these against your rights and process the minimum data needed. You may object at any time (see section 7).

4. Recipients

Diagnostics are processed on our behalf by Google (Firebase Crashlytics and Google Analytics for Firebase), acting as our processor. We do not sell your data or share it with any other third party.

5. International transfers

Google may process the above data on servers outside your country, including in the United States. Where data is transferred outside the EEA/UK/Switzerland, Google relies on appropriate safeguards, including the EU Standard Contractual Clauses and the EU–US Data Privacy Framework. See Google's Privacy Policy: policies.google.com/privacy.

6. Retention

Crash diagnostics are retained by Firebase Crashlytics for up to 90 days. Analytics data is retained per Firebase's configured retention (by default up to 2 months for user/event-level data; aggregated reports may be kept longer). We keep personal data no longer than necessary for the purposes above.

7. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to: access your data; request rectification or erasure; request restriction of processing; data portability; and to object to processing based on our legitimate interests. To exercise any of these, contact us at the address in section 1. Because the diagnostics use device-level identifiers rather than your name, we may ask for information to locate the relevant data.

8. Right to complain

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a data protection supervisory authority, in particular in the EU/EEA member state of your residence, place of work, or where an alleged infringement occurred (in Switzerland, the FDPIC).

9. Is providing this data required?

No. Providing diagnostics is not a statutory or contractual requirement and is not needed to use PvPeek's core features. The app's analysis works regardless; diagnostics only help us maintain and improve it, and you may object to or opt out of them.

10. Automated decision-making

We do not carry out automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal or similarly significant effects on you (GDPR Art. 22). The Pokémon analysis is a calculation performed on-device and is not used to make decisions about you.

11. Children

PvPeek is not directed to children under 13 (or the applicable age in your country) and we do not knowingly collect their data.

12. Changes

We may update this policy; the "last updated" date above will change. Material changes will be reflected in the app.
