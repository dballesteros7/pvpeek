Privacy Policy — PvPeek

Last updated: 15 June 2026

The short version: what's on your screen never leaves your phone. The app reads a single screen frame only when you tap the button, analyses it on-device, and never stores or transmits any image or its text. It sends only anonymous crash and usage diagnostics to help fix bugs and improve accuracy.

What the app does

PvPeek shows a floating button. When you tap it on a Pokémon's Appraisal screen, it captures one frame of your screen, reads the CP, name, IV bars and type on-device, and shows the Pokémon's PvP rank and recommended moves. The captured image is processed in memory and immediately discarded — it is never saved, uploaded, or shared.

Information we do NOT collect

  • No screenshots, screen recordings, or any captured image.
  • No text read from your screen (OCR results stay on-device).
  • No precise or coarse location, no contacts, no photos library.
  • No account, sign-in, email, or personal identifiers.

Information we do collect (anonymous diagnostics)

Through Google Firebase, the app collects:

  • Crash diagnostics (Firebase Crashlytics): stack traces, device model, OS version, and app version, so we can fix crashes.
  • Usage analytics (Firebase Analytics): for each analysis, a derived event with only these fields — outcome (success/failure), where the name was read from, how many types were detected, whether CP was read, a device-resolution bucket, and the recognised Pokémon species name. This contains no images, screen text, location, or identity.

Permissions and why

  • Display over other apps — to show the floating button.
  • Screen capture — to read a single frame when you tap; never continuous.
  • Notifications — Android requires a notification for the background service.
  • Internet — to send the anonymous diagnostics above to Firebase.

Third-party services

We use Google Firebase (Crashlytics and Analytics). Their handling of data is governed by Google's Privacy Policy: policies.google.com/privacy. Diagnostic data is retained per Firebase's default retention settings.

Children

The app is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.
