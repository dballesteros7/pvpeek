Privacy Policy — PvPeek

Last updated: 15 June 2026

In short: the contents of your screen — the captured image and the text read from it — never leave your phone. PvPeek reads a single frame only when you tap, analyses it entirely on your device, and discards it. It sends crash reports to help fix bugs and, only if you opt in, usage analytics that don't identify you by name. This notice is written to comply with major privacy laws, including the EU and UK GDPR, the Swiss FADP, and California's CCPA/CPRA.

What stays on your phone

To analyse a Pokémon, PvPeek captures one frame of your screen (only when you tap) and reads it on your device using on-device text recognition (Google ML Kit, which sends nothing off the device). The image and the text read from it — including any CP, IVs, and the catch date/location shown on screen — are processed in memory and immediately discarded; they are never stored, uploaded, or shared. We don't collect screenshots, screen contents, your location, contacts, photos, or any account. The only screen-derived value that may leave your device is the recognised Pokémon species name, and only if you opt in to analytics.

Categories of personal information (for US state-law readers)

The data above falls under two CCPA/CPRA categories: identifiers (the Firebase installation / app-instance identifiers) and internet or device activity (device model, OS/app version, and the in-app usage events). It is collected directly from your device through your use of the app. We do not collect sensitive personal information, and we do not sell or share personal information.
